**Executive Summary:** These proposals are designed to bridge the gap between emergency assistance and long-term economic independence. By focusing on the "3 Bases" of social stability—**Literacy, Numeracy, and Digital Fluency**—Guardian Angel Settlement Association (GASA) can empower families to navigate the modern world with confidence and self-sufficiency.

== 1. Adult & General Tutoring: The "3 Bases" Program

*   **Vision:** To provide a safe, non-judgmental environment for adults to master the fundamental skills required for daily life and employment.
*   **Core Strategy:** Focusing on the three foundations of understanding:
    ** **Literacy:** Reading comprehension for contracts, medical forms, and news.
    ** **Numeracy:** Practical mathematics for budgeting, shopping, and understanding bills.
    ** **Expression:** Basic writing for correspondence and personal documentation.
*   **Target Audience:** Adults who require foundational support to gain confidence in their professional and personal interactions.
*   **Anticipated Impact:** Improved job readiness and increased self-advocacy in legal and medical settings.

== 2. Digital Literacy Workshop: Bridging the "Working Age" Gap

*   **Vision:** To empower non-elderly adults with the essential digital tools required for the modern workforce.
*   **Core Strategy:** Practical training in essential digital navigation:
    ** **Professional Communication:** Email management, professional account setup, and identifying digital threats (phishing).
    ** **Safe Navigation:** Accessing government portals (IRS, MO.gov), banking, and school systems.
    ** **Employment Systems:** Navigating online job applications and basic word processing for resumes/cover letters.
*   **Target Audience:** Adults aged 25–55 who lack consistent access to technology or formal digital training.
*   **Anticipated Impact:** Increased employment opportunities and efficient access to essential online services.

== 3. "Budget to Build": Pantry-to-Table Nutrition & Finance

*   **Vision:** A dual-purpose workshop leveraging GASA's Social Stability food pantry to foster healthy living and financial discipline.
*   **Core Strategy:** Integrating resource management with practical life skills:
    ** **Live Demonstration:** A "Pantry-to-Table" session showing how to prepare a nutritious family meal using staples.
    ** **Financial Mapping:** A simplified exercise to identify small monthly savings opportunities within a fixed budget.
    ** **Empowerment Kits:** Take-home recipe cards and "Value Shopping" guides.
*   **Target Audience:** Current food pantry members and families seeking to maximize limited resources.
*   **Anticipated Impact:** Reduced food insecurity and improved household financial stability.

== 4. Family "Read & Play": Multigenerational Literacy Morning

*   **Vision:** To reinforce the "3 Bases" of learning within the home by engaging both parents and children simultaneously.
*   **Core Strategy:** Fostering a culture of learning through interactive play:
    ** **Interactive Literacy:** Parents learn techniques to engage children in reading through play-based storytime.
    ** **Numeracy Games:** Using play (blocks, flashcards) to introduce mathematical concepts at home with zero cost.
    ** **Resource Exchange:** A community book exchange to ensure every home has high-quality reading material.
*   **Target Audience:** Families enrolled in the North Campus Child Development Center (Infants to Age 5).
*   **Anticipated Impact:** Long-term educational success for the child and increased parental engagement in early literacy.
